Visitor Analysis

Target Audience

The target audience for Facebook is very broad. Typically, I feel as if they cater to a younger audience, those in their late teens to early twenties. The users may tend to not be overly wealthy; it is a free site and attracts most commonly college students. Geographically, their target audience is those in the United States. However, they do also cater to the needs of those outside of the U.S., but I would say the majority of their target audience resides within the United States.

Facebook meets the needs of the visitor by showing them what they came to the site for, which is typically to go through their news feed to see what is happening in the lives of those around them. It keeps it simple without showing too many controls that get in the way of the already small smartphone screen.

The content was organized with the navigation bar at the top, showing the key navigation points and then a drop down menu to be able to choose from less commonly used navigation points.

The mobile site was enjoyable because of its simplicity and its huge similarity with the actual website. You could transition seamlessly without any problems.

Facebook.com seemed to facilitate people in a mobile environment. It showed just the key information that someone would want to know while they were on-the-go, and provided quick viewing.
